Background

Earthquakes are one of the major natural disasters threatening Seattle. If a high-magnitude earthquake were to happen, there are over 800 buildings at risk to fall down and 46,000 households could be trapped in their homes (Seattle Office of Emergency Management, 2014). Rescue teams could spend days searching for survivors, trying to be as quiet as possible to hear any signs of life because they have no idea where survivors are nor how many are trapped in ruins. In the 2008 Sichuan Earthquake, 87,587 people died and an appalling 18,392 people are still missing. While rescue teams spend much time searching through empty houses lives fade away.

Design Question

Based on this situation, we define our design question around how can we minimize threats from natural disaster to the health of civilians living in the Greater Seattle Area and subsequently increase their chances of survival?
