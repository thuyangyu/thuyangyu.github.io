Prototype

Scenario 1 - User’s Home


It was 4:37 PDT in the morning and Darren was vast asleep enjoying his dream. Unexpectedly, a M-8.0 earthquake hit the Seattle area. Darren felt the shake through his sleep, woke up and hid un- der his bed. Unfortunately, the earthquake was so strong that Darren’s home collapsed leaving him buried under the ruins. Luckily, Darren had previously equipped his home with “Beacon” and provid- ed the network with his location, user type, household and house specific information through their mobile App. The device recorded that he was at home at the time of the earthquake and sent that data to the Beacon Network. This meant that the rescue teams could see his device’s “help” call so Darren knew that rescue teams will come to him. The thought of someone knowing about his situa- tion comforted Darren and he was now patiently waiting for the rescue team members to come and save him.

Scenario 2 - Beacon Monitoring Office

It was 7:39 EST in the morning at the Beacon central monitoring office in New York. The monitor-

ing employee on duty was comfortably drinking their coffee when they suddenly received an alarm from the Beacon network. The alert showed an M8.0 earthquake in the Seattle area and help calls from thousands of Beacon clients in the area. The employee quickly reminds themselves of their training and the steps they now need to take to ensure timely help for their customers. They start monitoring the situation in Seattle using the data provided through the Beacon network. They check the scope of the situation, the total number of devices, the number of affected people and other data coming through. The employee keeps an eye on the map of the disaster zone and prepares to recruit any extra help from outside sources in case it comes apparent that the local rescue groups won’t make it everywhere. Some of the “help” calls on the map start switching off as people report back their status as “safe”. After an hour passes more than half of the area has still not been claimed by

a rescue group as their work region so the employee makes a call to a nearby rescue unit that’s out- side the direct impact zone to ask them for assistance with the rescue missions.

Scenario 3 - Rescue Team


It was 4:58 PDT in Seattle, the captain of the local rescue team Ryan received emergency mission re- quest from the local authorities. He was provided with substantial mission information that included victims’ locations and household information. Captain Ryan marked the work area for his team in the Beacon system, gathered the team and equipped them with rescue team terminals of the Bea- con system. The team arrived at 6:00 am and started locating victims according to the data provided by the Beacon system. They got the trapped people out of the ruins one by one. After each ruin site got cleared, Ryan updated the statuses of the Beacon subscribers in that area.

For the prototype, we focused on the core scenario, which is, to survive people. This is a tablet app prototype for the rescue team member, which includes 4 pages: task list page, task detail pages, task on map page and mission log page. We use Balsamiq, an interactive mockup tool to create this lean prototype, that is enough to do the prototype evaluation.
